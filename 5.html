Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a branch of computer science that focuses on creating intelligent machines and systems capable of performing tasks that normally require human intelligence. These tasks include learning, reasoning, problem-solving, understanding language, recognizing images and speech, and making decisions. AI uses data, algorithms, and advanced computing techniques to analyze information and improve its performance over time. The idea behind AI is to enable machines to think, learn, and adapt to new situations. Unlike traditional computer programs that only follow fixed instructions, AI systems can learn from experience and become more accurate as they process more data.

How AI Works

Artificial Intelligence works by collecting and analyzing large amounts of data. AI models identify patterns, make predictions, and improve their performance through continuous learning. Technologies such as machine learning, deep learning, and natural language processing help AI systems understand information and interact with humans more effectively.

Types of Artificial Intelligence.

  1. Narrow AI (Weak AI)

    2. Narrow AI (Weak AI) is a type of Artificial Intelligence that is designed and trained to perform a specific task or a limited set of tasks. Unlike humans, it cannot think independently or perform activities outside its programmed area. It works within predefined boundaries and focuses only on the tasks it has been created to do. Narrow AI is the most common and widely used form of AI today. It powers many everyday technologies such as virtual assistants, recommendation systems, translation tools, facial recognition systems, and spam filters. Although it can perform certain tasks very efficiently, it does not possess human-like understanding, emotions, or self-awareness.
    Example:
    • Virtual assistants.
    • Chatbots.
    • Recommendation systems.
    • Email spam filters.
    • Translation applications.

  2. General AI (Strong AI)

    3. General AI (Strong AI) is a theoretical type of Artificial Intelligence that would have the ability to understand, learn, reason, and perform any intellectual task that a human being can do. Unlike Narrow AI, which is limited to specific tasks, General AI would be capable of adapting to different situations, solving new problems, and applying knowledge across multiple areas without needing separate programming for every task. The main goal of General AI is to create machines that can think, learn from experience, make independent decisions, and understand complex concepts in a human-like manner. If developed successfully, General AI could work in various fields such as healthcare, education, scientific research, engineering, and business with a level of flexibility similar to humans. However, General AI does not currently exist. It remains a research goal, and scientists and engineers around the world are still working to achieve this level of intelligence.
    Note: General AI is still under research and has not been successfully developed yet.

  3. Super AI

    4. Super AI (Super Artificial Intelligence) is a hypothetical and future concept of Artificial Intelligence that would surpass human intelligence in every aspect. It would be able to think, learn, reason, solve problems, make decisions, and create new ideas much faster and more effectively than humans. Super AI is expected to have advanced abilities such as understanding complex situations, adapting to new environments, and performing tasks with exceptional accuracy. Unlike Narrow AI and General AI, Super AI would not only match human intelligence but could exceed it in areas such as scientific research, creativity, strategic planning, and decision-making. However, Super AI does not currently exist and remains a theoretical concept. Researchers continue to discuss its possibilities, benefits, and potential risks.
    Examples (Theoretical):
    • An AI system that can outperform humans in every field.
    • A super-intelligent machine capable of solving major global problems such as climate change and complex diseases.
    • An AI that can independently create new technologies and scientific discoveries.

Based on Functionality

  • Reactive Machines

    • Reactive Machines are the most basic type of Artificial Intelligence. They are designed to respond only to current information and cannot store memories or learn from past experiences. These AI systems analyze the present situation and make decisions based only on the data available at that moment. Reactive Machines follow predefined rules and cannot improve their performance through experience. They do not understand emotions, predict future events, or remember previous interactions. Because of these limitations, they are used for specific tasks where immediate responses are required.
    Examples:
    • IBM Deep Blue (the chess computer that defeated a world champion).
    • Basic chess-playing AI programs.
    • Simple rule-based game systems.

  • Limited Memory AI

    • Limited Memory AI is a type of Artificial Intelligence that can learn from past data and use that information to make better decisions. Unlike Reactive Machines, this AI can temporarily store information, analyze previous experiences, and improve its performance over time. Most modern AI systems today are Limited Memory AI because they use historical data, patterns, and observations to provide accurate results. However, the stored information is usually limited to a specific purpose and is not the same as human memory. Limited Memory AI is widely used in many industries because it can adapt to changing situations and make smarter decisions based on previous data.
    Examples:
    • Self-driving cars.
    • Recommendation systems (movies, music, online shopping).
    • Virtual assistants.
    • Fraud detection systems.

  • Theory of Mind AI
    • Theory of Mind AI is a future type of Artificial Intelligence that is expected to understand human emotions, thoughts, beliefs, intentions, and social interactions. The goal of this AI is to communicate and interact with people in a more human-like way by recognizing how people feel and responding appropriately. Unlike current AI systems, Theory of Mind AI would not only process data but also understand emotions and behavior before making decisions. This would allow machines to build better relationships with humans and work more naturally alongside them. At present, Theory of Mind AI has not been fully developed and remains a research concept.
    Examples (Theoretical):
    • A robot that can understand a person's emotions and adjust its behavior accordingly.
    • An AI teacher that recognizes when a student is confused and changes its teaching style.
    • An AI healthcare assistant that understands a patient's emotional state while providing support.

  • Self-Aware AI

    • Self-Aware AI is a hypothetical and the most advanced type of Artificial Intelligence. It is a future concept in which an AI system would have its own consciousness, self-awareness, emotions, and understanding of its own existence. This means the machine would not only process information but would also be aware of itself, its actions, and its surroundings. Unlike current AI systems, Self-Aware AI would be able to think independently, understand human emotions, form its own opinions, and make decisions without relying entirely on human instructions. It would have intelligence comparable to, or potentially beyond, human intelligence. However, Self-Aware AI does not exist today and remains a theoretical concept that researchers discuss and study.
    Examples (Theoretical):
    • A robot that is aware of its own existence and can make independent choices.
    • An AI system that understands its emotions, goals, and actions.
    • A machine that can think and interact exactly like a human being.
